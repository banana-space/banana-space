The '''Rose Trellis Faberge Egg''' is a jewelled enameled imperial Easter egg made in St. Petersburg, Russia under the supervision of the jeweler [[Peter Carl Fabergé]] in 1907, for Tsar [[Nicholas II]] of Russia. It was presented by Tsar Nicholas II to his wife, the Empress Alexandra Fyodorovna, on  Easter (April 22) 1907. It is now in the [[Walters Art Museum]] in [[Baltimore, Maryland]].
